🇪🇸 ser vs estar – both mean "to be", but they are not the same

In Spanish, there are two verbs that translate as "to be". Choosing the right one changes the meaning of a sentence. This page explains the difference with simple rules and examples.

📌 quick overview

  • SER → used for permanent or defining characteristics: identity, origin, time, profession, inherent qualities.
  • ESTAR → used for temporary states, locations, and conditions (feelings, positions, results of an action).

🧩 conjugation – present tense

🔹 SER (to be)

  • yo soy
  • tú eres
  • él/ella/usted es
  • nosotros/nosotras somos
  • vosotros/vosotras sois (Spain)
  • ellos/ellas/ustedes son

🔸 ESTAR (to be)

  • yo estoy
  • tú estás
  • él/ella/usted está
  • nosotros/nosotras estamos
  • vosotros/vosotras estáis (Spain)
  • ellos/ellas/ustedes están

🧠 when to use SER (D.O.T.I.P.) – helpful acronym

  • Date / day / time → Hoy es lunes (Today is Monday); Son las tres (It’s three o’clock).
  • Origin / nationality → Soy de México (I’m from Mexico); Ella es española (She is Spanish).
  • Traits / characteristics (permanent / inherent) → El cielo es azul (The sky is blue); Juan es alto (Juan is tall).
  • Identity / profession / relationship → Ella es doctora (She is a doctor); Él es mi hermano (He is my brother).
  • Possession / material / ownership → El coche es de Pedro (The car is Pedro’s); La mesa es de madera (The table is made of wood).

💧 when to use ESTAR (P.L.A.C.E.) – helpful acronym

  • Position / posture → Estamos sentados (We are sitting); El libro está encima de la mesa (The book is on the table).
  • Location (where something/someone is) → Madrid está en España (Madrid is in Spain); ¿Dónde está el baño? (Where is the bathroom?).
  • Action in progress (gerund) → Estoy comiendo (I am eating); Ella está durmiendo (She is sleeping).
  • Condition / state (temporary) → El café está frío (The coffee is cold); Hoy estoy cansado (Today I am tired).
  • Emotion / feeling → Estamos felices (We are happy); ¿Por qué estás triste? (Why are you sad?).

⚖️ ser vs estar – meaning change with adjectives

Some adjectives change meaning completely depending on whether you use ser or estar. Here are the most famous examples:

  • ser bueno = to be good (morally / at something) — estar bueno = to be tasty / attractive (colloquial)
  • ser malo = to be bad (evil / poor quality) — estar malo = to be ill / spoiled (food)
  • ser listo = to be clever / smart — estar listo = to be ready
  • ser vivo = to be sharp / lively — estar vivo = to be alive
  • ser aburrido = to be boring (personality) — estar aburrido = to be bored (feeling)
  • ser seguro = to be safe (reliable, secure) — estar seguro = to be sure / certain (opinion)
  • ser rico = to be rich — estar rico = to be tasty / delicious
  • ser verde = to be green (color, inexperienced) — estar verde = to be unripe / not ready
  • ser orgulloso = to be proud (as a personality trait) — estar orgulloso = to be proud (of a specific thing)

📋 side‑by‑side comparison – same sentence, different verb

  • Él es aburrido. → He is a boring person. (personality)
  • Él está aburrido. → He is bored. (right now, feeling)
  • La manzana es verde. → The apple is green (by nature, variety).
  • La manzana está verde. → The apple is unripe (temporary state).
  • Soy casado. → (less common) can mean “I am married” as a trait, but normally we say soy casado is accepted, though estoy casado emphasizes current state. Usually, marriage is seen as a state, so estoy casado is more typical.
  • Estoy casado. → I am married (current status).

🧪 mini self‑test – choose ser or estar

  1. Mi hermana _______ inteligente. (My sister is smart.)
  2. Nosotros _______ en el parque. (We are at the park.)
  3. _______ las dos de la tarde. (It is 2 PM.)
  4. La sopa _______ muy caliente. (The soup is very hot – temperature.)
  5. ¿De dónde _______ tú? (Where are you from?)
  6. Los niños _______ durmiendo. (The children are sleeping.)
  7. Este reloj _______ de mi abuelo. (This watch is my grandfather’s.)
  8. Hoy _______ muy contenta. (Today I am very happy.)

answers: 1. es, 2. estamos, 3. Son, 4. está, 5. eres, 6. están, 7. es, 8. estoy

✅ final tip

SER = essence, identity, what makes something what it is.
ESTAR = state, condition, location, how something is at a given moment.

Learn the rules, but also listen to real Spanish — the difference becomes natural with practice.
