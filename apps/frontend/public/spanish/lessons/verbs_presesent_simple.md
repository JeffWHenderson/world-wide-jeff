# Present tense

Conjugating present simple in Spanish

All verbs in Spanish have an infinitive form. This is the “to-” equivalent in English that you see in dictionaries,
like “to speak” (hablar), “to eat” (comer), and “to live” (vivir).

Each of these infinitive verbs ends in either -ar, -er, or -ir. The trick to conjugating regular verbs in
Spanish is to take off these endings and replace them with their correct ending, as seen in the table below.

| Present simple    | -ar   | -er   | -ir |
| :---------------- | :---- | :---- | :-- |
| yo                | -o    | -o    |  -o |
| tú                | -as   | -es   | -es |
| él, ella, usted   | -a    | -a    | -e  |
| nosotros          | -amos | -emos | -imos |
| vosotros          | -áis  |  -éis | -ís |
| Ellos, ellas, ustedes | -an |  -en | -en |

So, if you want to say “I speak,” then you take hablar, remove its -ar ending, and add -o on the end. This gives you hablo!

I speak English and Spanish → Hablo inglés y español.

Once you’ve got the hang of it, test yourself with the three verbs hablar, comer, and vivir.

I live → yo + vivir → vivo

You eat → tú + comer → comes

She speaks → ella + hablar → habla

We eat → nosotros + comer → comimos

You (plural) live → vosotros + vivir → vivís

They speak → Ellos + hablar → hablan
