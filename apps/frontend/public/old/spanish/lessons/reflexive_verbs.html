🇪🇸 reflexive verbs in Spanish (verbos reflexivos)

Reflexive verbs are used when the subject does an action to or for themselves. In English we say “I wash myself” – in Spanish that’s yo me lavo. The key is the little reflexive pronoun (me, te, se…) that matches the subject.

📌 1. reflexive pronouns – the pieces you need

Every reflexive verb has two parts: the verb + a reflexive pronoun that goes before the conjugated verb (or attached to an infinitive/gerund).

  • me – (myself)
  • te – (yourself – informal tú)
  • se – (himself, herself, yourself – formal usted)
  • nos – (ourselves)
  • os – (yourselves – vosotros, Spain)
  • se – (themselves, yourselves – ustedes)

🧩 2. how to conjugate a reflexive verb (example: lavarse – to wash oneself)

Step 1: remove the -se ending → you get lavar.
Step 2: conjugate lavar in the present tense (regular -ar).
Step 3: put the correct reflexive pronoun in front.

  • yo me lavo – I wash myself
  • tú te lavas – you wash yourself
  • él/ella/usted se lava – he/she/you wash(es) himself/herself/yourself
  • nosotros/nosotras nos lavamos – we wash ourselves
  • vosotros/vosotras os laváis – you all wash yourselves (Spain)
  • ellos/ellas/ustedes se lavan – they/you all wash themselves/yourselves

notice: the pronoun always matches the subject, and it’s placed right before the verb.

🔄 3. common reflexive verbs (daily routine & body care)

  • levantarse – to get up
  • sentarse (e→ie) – to sit down
  • llamarse – to be called / to call oneself
  • vestirse (e→i) – to get dressed
  • despertarse (e→ie) – to wake up
  • acostarse (o→ue) – to go to bed
  • ducharse – to shower
  • bañarse – to bathe / take a bath
  • cepillarse (los dientes / el pelo) – to brush (one’s teeth / hair)
  • peinarse – to comb one’s hair
  • maquillarse – to put on makeup
  • afeitarse – to shave
  • preocuparse (por) – to worry (about)
  • sentirse (e→ie) – to feel

⚠️ 4. verbs that change meaning when reflexive

Some verbs exist in both reflexive and non‑reflexive forms, but the meaning changes.

  • ir – to go / irse – to leave / go away
  • levantar – to lift (something) / levantarse – to get up
  • poner – to put / ponerse – to put on (clothes) / to become (e.g. ponerse nervioso)
  • dormir – to sleep / dormirse – to fall asleep
  • quedar – to meet / to be located / quedarse – to stay (in a place)
  • llamar – to call / llamarse – to be named
  • acordar – to agree / acordarse (de) – to remember
  • despedir – to fire (from a job) / despedirse (de) – to say goodbye (to)

🧪 5. where to put the pronoun? (two common positions)

A) before a conjugated verb (most common)

  • Ella se levanta tarde. – She gets up late.
  • Nosotros nos acostamos temprano. – We go to bed early.

B) attached to an infinitive, gerund, or affirmative command

  • Voy a levantarme temprano. – I’m going to get up early. (infinitive)
  • Estoy bañándome. – I am bathing myself. (gerund – add accent to keep stress)
  • Siéntate por favor. – Sit down please. (affirmative tú command)

Note: With negative commands, the pronoun goes before: No te sientes. (Don’t sit down.)

📘 6. examples in context (daily routine)

  • ¿A qué hora te despiertas? – What time do you wake up?
  • Me despierto a las siete, pero me levanto a las siete y media. – I wake up at seven, but I get up at seven thirty.
  • Luego me ducho, me visto y me voy al trabajo. – Then I shower, get dressed, and leave for work.
  • Mi hermana se maquilla antes de salir. – My sister puts on makeup before going out.
  • Por la noche, nos acostamos sobre las once. – At night, we go to bed around eleven.

✅ 7. reflexive verbs are not optional – they carry meaning

In English we often drop “myself” (I wash, I get up), but in Spanish the pronoun is essential. Compare:

  • Lavo el coche. – I wash the car. (direct object = car)
  • Me lavo. – I wash myself. (reflexive – the action returns to me)

If you say lavo without an object, it sounds incomplete. The pronoun makes it clear who receives the action.

🧠 8. quick practice – fill in the correct reflexive pronoun

  1. Yo _______ siento feliz. (I feel happy.)
  2. Ellos _______ llaman García. (They are called García.)
  3. ¿Tú _______ acuestas tarde? (Do you go to bed late?)
  4. Nosotros _______ ponemos nerviosos antes de un examen. (We get nervous before an exam.)
  5. Mi padre _______ afeita cada mañana. (My father shaves every morning.)
  6. Vosotros _______ divertís mucho en la fiesta. (You guys have fun at the party.)
  7. Usted _______ preocupa demasiado. (You worry too much – formal.)

answers: 1. me, 2. se, 3. te, 4. nos, 5. se, 6. os, 7. se

📌 summary: reflexive verbs always use a reflexive pronoun that matches the subject. They describe actions done to oneself, especially daily routines. Learn them with the pronoun — levantarse, despertarse, sentirse — and they’ll become natural.
