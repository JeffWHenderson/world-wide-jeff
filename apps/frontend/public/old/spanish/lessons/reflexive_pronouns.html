🇪🇸 reflexive pronouns in Spanish (pronombres reflexivos)

Reflexive pronouns are the little words like me, te, se that show the action of a verb reflects back on the subject. They always agree with the subject and are essential for reflexive verbs. This lesson focuses only on the pronouns themselves: their forms, where they go, and how they work.

📌 1. the six reflexive pronouns

Each grammatical person has its own reflexive pronoun. Memorise these – they never change (no masculine/feminine forms except for nos/os which stay the same).

  • me – myself (1st person singular: yo)
  • te – yourself (2nd person singular, informal: tú)
  • se – himself, herself, yourself (formal), itself (3rd person singular: él, ella, usted)
  • nos – ourselves (1st person plural: nosotros, nosotras)
  • os – yourselves (2nd person plural, informal Spain: vosotros, vosotras)
  • se – themselves, yourselves (3rd person plural: ellos, ellas, ustedes)

Note: "se" is used for both singular and plural in the 3rd person – context tells you who.

🧩 2. what does "reflexive" mean?

A pronoun is reflexive when the subject and the object of the verb are the same person. The pronoun receives the action.

  • English example: "I see myself in the mirror." → myself = reflexive pronoun.
  • Spanish: Yo me veo en el espejo. → me = reflexive pronoun, and it refers back to yo.

Without the reflexive pronoun, the verb would affect someone/something else:
Veo a Juan (I see Juan) – not reflexive. Me veo (I see myself) – reflexive.

⚙️ 3. placement of reflexive pronouns – two common positions

Unlike in English, Spanish reflexive pronouns usually come before the conjugated verb. But they can also attach to infinitives, gerunds, or affirmative commands.

A) BEFORE a conjugated verb (most common)

  • Me llamo Ana. – I call myself Ana.
  • ¿A qué hora te levantas? – What time do you get (yourself) up?
  • Ella se peina despacio. – She combs her hair (herself) slowly.
  • Nosotros nos sentimos bien. – We feel (ourselves) well.
  • Ellos se van ahora. – They leave (themselves) now.

B) ATTACHED to an infinitive, gerund, or affirmative command

  • Infinitive: Vas a levantarte. – You are going to get (yourself) up. (also possible: te vas a levantar – pronoun before conjugated verb)
  • Gerund: Está bañándose. – He is bathing himself. (add an accent to keep original stress: baˈñandose → baˈñándose)
  • Affirmative command: Siéntate. – Sit (yourself) down. Lávense las manos. – Wash (yourselves) your hands.

With negative commands, the pronoun goes BEFORE: No te sientes. – Don’t sit down.

🔁 4. reflexive pronouns vs. other pronouns (direct/indirect)

Don’t confuse reflexive pronouns with direct object pronouns (lo, la, los, las) or indirect object pronouns (le, les). Reflexive pronouns always refer to the subject.

  • Reflexive (me): Yo me lavo. → I wash myself.
  • Direct object (me – same form but different function): Él me ve. → He sees me. (I am not doing the action to myself; “me” receives the action from “él”.)

But in the 3rd person, the reflexive pronoun is se, while direct object pronouns are lo/la and indirect are le. So se is uniquely reflexive (except for reciprocal actions).

🤝 5. reciprocal use – "each other"

With plural subjects, the reflexive pronouns nos, os, se can also mean “each other”. This is still reflexive grammar, but the meaning is reciprocal.

  • Nos escribimos cartas. – We write letters to each other.
  • ¿Os queréis? – Do you love each other? (vosotros)
  • Ellos se ayudan siempre. – They always help each other.

Context tells you whether it’s reflexive (self) or reciprocal (each other). For example, se lavan could mean “they wash themselves” or “they wash each other” – usually it’s clear from situation.

📋 6. full conjugation of a reflexive verb (lavarse) – showing pronoun + verb

Notice how the pronoun changes with the person, but the verb ending also changes:

  • yo me lavo
  • tú te lavas
  • él/ella/usted se lava
  • nosotros/nosotras nos lavamos
  • vosotros/vosotras os laváis
  • ellos/ellas/ustedes se lavan

🧠 7. mini practice – choose the correct reflexive pronoun

Fill in the blank with me, te, se, nos, os.

  1. Yo _______ despierto a las seis.
  2. Ellos _______ visten rápido.
  3. ¿Tú _______ llamas Carlos?
  4. Nosotras _______ maquillamos antes de salir.
  5. El gato _______ lava las patas.
  6. Vosotros _______ acostáis muy tarde.
  7. Usted _______ preocupa demasiado.
  8. Mi hermano y yo _______ queremos mucho. (reciprocal)

answers: 1. me, 2. se, 3. te, 4. nos, 5. se, 6. os, 7. se, 8. nos

✅ summary – key points about reflexive pronouns

  • They always match the subject: me, te, se, nos, os, se.
  • They usually go before a conjugated verb: me levanto.
  • They can attach to infinitives, gerunds, and affirmative commands: levantarse, levantándose, levántate.
  • In negative commands, they go before: no te levantes.
  • With plural subjects, they can mean “each other” (reciprocal).

Master the pronouns, and you master the core of reflexive verbs. They are small but mighty!
