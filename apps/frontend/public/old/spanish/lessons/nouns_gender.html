How nouns have gender in Spanish

In Spanish, every noun has a gender — it is either masculine or feminine. This is a grammatical feature, not directly related to biological sex (though words for people/animals often follow sex).

🔹 basic rules of thumb

  • Masculine nouns usually end in -o.
    el libro (book), el perro (dog), el vaso (glass).
  • Feminine nouns usually end in -a.
    la mesa (table), la casa (house), la silla (chair).

🔸 common exceptions

  • Nouns ending in -ma, -pa, -ta that come from Greek are often masculine:
    el problema (problem), el mapa (map), el planeta (planet).
  • Nouns ending in -ción, -sión, -dad, -tad, -tud are feminine:
    la canción (song), la televisión (television), la libertad (liberty), la actitud (attitude).
  • Nouns ending in -e or a consonant can be either — you have to learn them with the article.
    el puente (bridge – masc.), la mente (mind – fem.), el árbol (tree – masc.), la pared (wall – fem.).

🧩 groups with fixed gender

  • Days of the week, months, numbers, and languages are masculine:
    el lunes, el enero, el cinco, el español.
  • Letters of the alphabet are feminine:
    la a, la be, la ce.
  • Most rivers, oceans, and lakes are masculine:
    el Amazonas, el Atlántico.
  • Islands and mountain ranges tend to be feminine:
    las Canarias, las Malvinas, los Pirineos (the Pyrenees — actually masculine because los montes is implied).

👤 nouns for people & animals

Many nouns that refer to people have both a masculine and a feminine form:

  • el amigo → la amiga (friend)
  • el profesor → la profesora (teacher)
  • el presidente → la presidenta (president)

Some are invariable (same form) and the gender is shown only by the article:

  • el estudiante / la estudiante (student)
  • el pianista / la pianista (pianist)

📌 why does gender matter?

Gender affects other words in the sentence:

  • Articles: el / la / un / una must match the noun.
  • Adjectives: they change ending to agree with the noun.
    el perro blanco (the white dog) – masculine
    la casa blanca (the white house) – feminine
  • Pronouns: lo / la / le also reflect gender.

⚠️ a few tricky pairs

Sometimes the same word changes meaning depending on gender:

  • el capital (money / capital) — la capital (capital city)
  • el cura (priest) — la cura (cure / treatment)
  • el orden (order, sequence) — la orden (command / religious order)
  • el papa (the Pope) — la papa (potato)

🧾 summary: There is no single rule that covers everything, but patterns help. Always learn a new noun with its article (el or la) — that way you remember the gender from the start.
